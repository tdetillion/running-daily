International
Audrey Werro (SUI) ran 1:53.80 at the Paris Diamond League on June 28 — the third-fastest time in history — becoming the first woman to break 1:54.00 twice in a career; many view her as owning the two fastest legitimate times in history given that the faster marks were products of the Eastern Bloc in the 1980s.
Three Diamond League records fell in Paris: Busang Collen Kebinatshipi (BOT) ran 43.54 in the men's 400m, Werro broke the women's 800m DL record, and Marileidy Paulino added a world-leading 48.48 in the women's 400m.
Cameron Myers (AUS), barely 20 years old, broke the Oceanian record with a world-leading 3:28.00 in the men's 1500m in Paris, dropping Azeddine Habz with 200 metres to go before winning by almost two seconds.
Noah Lyles took a quarter of a second off the world 150m best at the Golden Spike in Ostrava on June 16, clocking 14.67 — the Olympic 100m champion and four-time world 200m champion was contesting the distance for the first time. World Athletics doesn't recognize world records in the 150m event.
France's Vincent Bouillard won the 2026 Western States 100-Mile in a course-record 13:46:15, moving into the lead in the final 15 miles and breaking Jim Walmsley's previous course record by 23 minutes and 12 seconds. American Jenn Lichter triumphed in her 100-mile debut, going under Courtney Dauwalter's previous course record and stopping the clock at 15:28:05.

United States
Grant Fisher (USA) won the men's 5000m in Paris with a big kick in 12:54.80, with Jacob Krop (KEN, 12:55.22) and Andreas Almgren (SWE, 12:55.38) right behind. It was his first career Diamond League win.
17-year-old Cooper Lutkenhaus remained undefeated in the 800m as a pro at Oslo, barely holding off World and Olympic champion Emmanuel Wanyonyi (KEN) in a world-leading 1:42.08, just 0.01 ahead.
Parker Valby ran 14:49.41 in her first outdoor race as a pro at the LA Track Fest (May 23), then followed up with 14:39.88 at the USATF LA Grand Prix on June 13 — the eighth-fastest time by an American in the event.
Reigning US 800m champion Jonah Koech, Olympic champion Athing Mu, and World Indoor record-holder Josh Hoey have all been MIA in 2026, with no confirmed return dates announced.
Germany's Frederik Ruppert became the first non-African to break 8:00 in the steeplechase, a barrier that had stood for decades against non-African runners.
